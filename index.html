    Why Statement Day & Due Date matter

    The two dates aren't the same — and the gap between them is where the savings live. Every credit card and most loans have a statement closing day (when your balance is reported and interest calculations begin) and a payment due date (the deadline to avoid late fees).

    Credit-score lift

    Card issuers report your balance to the bureaus on the statement closing day, not the due date. Pay it down before statement closes and the bureaus see lower utilization — typically a 20-50 FICO point swing.

    Interest savings

    Cards charge interest from the purchase date if you carry a balance past the due date — paying full statement balance in time keeps your grace period alive and your interest at $0.

    Loans accrue daily

    Most loans (student, auto, mortgage) accrue interest every day. Paying ~7 days early on a $30K balance at 6.8% APR saves about $140/year — for free.

    Cascade effect

    Higher FICO unlocks better refinance rates. A score jump from 690 → 740 can cut your auto-loan APR by 2-3 points — thousands saved over the life of the loan.

    Click Add at the top → choose Credit Card or Loan → fill in Statement closes and Payment due. WJP will mark your optimal pay-by date and run the savings math automatically.

    How to Export Your Credit Report
    💚
    Credit Karma
    1. Go to creditkarma.com and log in
    2. Navigate to Credit Cards → My Cards
    3. Scroll down and click "Manage Accounts"
    4. For each account, click the 3-dot menu → "Export transactions"
    5. Save the .csv file and import it here
    💡 Credit Karma CSV includes: Account Name, Balance, Credit Limit, APR, Payment Due Date
    🔵
    Experian
    1. Log in at experian.com
    2. Go to Credit Report → Full Credit Report
    3. Click "Download Your Report"
    4. Choose CSV format (not PDF)
    5. Import the downloaded file here
    💡 Experian CSV includes: Account Name, Balance, High Credit, Monthly Payment, Status
    🏦
    Bank / Generic CSV

    The import engine can auto-detect any CSV that contains columns like:

    Name / Account Balance APR / Interest Rate Min Payment Due Date
